Warm Up

Description: Jumping jacks are a classic full-body warm-up exercise. This cardio movement helps elevate your heart rate and prepares your muscles for more intense exercise. It also engages the legs, arms, and core.

How to do it: Stand with your feet together and arms at your sides. Jump and simultaneously spread your legs wide while raising your arms overhead. Quickly return to the starting position. Repeat for 30-60 seconds

Benefits: Improves cardiovascular fitness, increases flexibility, and warms up the whole body.

Description: Arm circles are a great warm-up for the shoulders and upper body. This movement helps increase blood flow to your upper body and mobilizes the shoulder joints.

How to do it: Extend your arms out to the sides, keeping them parallel to the ground. Start making small circles with your arms, gradually increasing the size of the circles. Do this for 30 seconds in one direction, then switch to the opposite direction.

Benefits: Improves shoulder mobility, increases circulation to the arms, and activates the upper body muscles.

Description: Hip circles are a great exercise to mobilize the hips and lower back. They also activate the core muscles and improve flexibility in the hip joint.

How to do it: Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and place your hands on your hips. Make circular motions with your hips in a clockwise direction for 30 seconds, then reverse the direction for 30 seconds.

Benefits: Enhances flexibility in the hips, strengthens the core, and prepares the lower body for movement.

Upper Body

Description: Push-ups are a classic upper body exercise that targets the chest, shoulders, and triceps. They also engage the core for stability.

How to do it: Start in a plank position with your hands placed slightly wider than shoulder-width apart. Lower your body by bending your elbows until your chest is just above the ground. Push through your palms to return to the starting position.

Benefits: Strengthens the chest, shoulders, triceps, and core. Helps with stability and upper body endurance.

Description: Tricep dips focus on the triceps, shoulders, and chest. This exercise can be performed using a bench or any elevated surface.

How to do it: Sit on the edge of a bench with your hands gripping the edge beside your hips. Slide your butt off the edge and extend your legs in front of you. Lower your body by bending your elbows until they are at a 90-degree angle, then push back up to the starting position.

Benefits: Targets the triceps, shoulders, and chest, improving upper body pushing strength.

Description: This exercise combines the plank and push-up into one movement, providing a great workout for the shoulders, triceps, and core.

How to do it: Start in a forearm plank position. Push up onto your hands, one hand at a time, then lower yourself back down onto your forearms. Alternate which hand pushes you up and which hand lowers you down.

Benefits: Strengthens the chest, triceps, shoulders, and core, improving stability and endurance.

Lower Body

Description: Squats are a fundamental lower body exercise that targets the quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes, and calves. This movement also engages the core for stability.

How to do it: Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and your toes slightly turned out. Lower your body by bending your knees and pushing your hips back, keeping your chest upright. Go as low as you can while maintaining good form, then push through your heels to return to standing.

Benefits: Strengthens the legs and glutes, enhances mobility in the hips and knees, and improves core stability.

Description: Lunges are a single-leg movement that targets the quads, hamstrings, glutes, and calves. They also improve balance and coordination.

How to do it: Stand with your feet together, then take a big step forward with one leg. Lower your hips until both knees are at 90-degree angles, ensuring your front knee doesn't go beyond your toes. Push off the front foot to return to the starting position and repeat on the other side.

Benefits: Strengthens the legs, glutes, and core, improves balance, and targets each leg individually.

Description: Glute bridges focus on the glutes, hamstrings, and lower back. This exercise helps improve hip extension and strengthens the posterior chain.

How to do it: Lie on your back with your knees bent and feet flat on the floor, hip-width apart. Press through your heels to lift your hips off the ground, squeezing your glutes at the top. Lower your hips back down without touching the floor and repeat.

Benefits: Strengthens the glutes, hamstrings, and lower back. It also helps improve posture and hip flexibility.
